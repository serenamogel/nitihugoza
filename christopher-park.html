Early Life

Christopher Park was born on 30 July 1987 in Bamberg, Upper Franconia, Germany. He grew up in Schloßborn with his parents. 

As of 2020, Christopher Park is 33 years old. He holds dual nationality, German and Korean. Park belongs to a mixed ethnical background. 

Regarding his education, he attended Taunus High School, located in the city of Königstein im Taunus. In 2007, Park completed his secondary education at Albert Einstein School. 

At the Saar University of Music, he began studying music with German Pianist Thomas Duis. After that, Christopher took classes from pianists like Lev Natochenny, Joachim Volkmann, and Lev Oborin. 

Career Highlights 

Christopher Park is known for his concerts at the Weilburgh Castle, the Summit Music Festival New York, the Rheingau Music Festival, and others. 

Moreover, he has also performed in concerts held in the United States, South Korea, South Africa, and Europe. Likewise, he has collaborated with renowned artists like Vladimir Krainev, Cyprien Katsaris, Karl-Heinz Kämmerling, and Gary Graffman. 

Some of his songs and albums are Russian Transcription (2010), I love you (2010), Preghiera (2011), Beethoven The Cello Sonatas - Solé Recordings (2016), and many others. 

Regarding his other achievements, Christopher Park received the sponsorship award from the Hauch & Aufhäuser Kulturstiftung. In 2011, he was even nominated for the first German Pianist Awards. 

A year later, he won a lottery prize at the Rheingau Music Festival. In addition, German Pianist Christopher Park was acknowledged by one of the Largest classical music awards, the Leonard Bernstein Award.
