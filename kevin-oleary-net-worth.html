Kevin OLeary Net Worth: Lifestyle &amp; Businesses

Kevin O’Leary has a net worth of $400 million. He is a Canadian businessman, politician, television personality, and author.

O’Leary is best known as the founder of software company SoftKey International. Similarly, he has appeared in several TV shows, including Shark Tank and Dragon Den.

SoftKey International sold family education and entertainment software, which later acquired several of its rival companies such as Compton’s New Media, Brøderbund, and The Learning Company.

However, the company changed its name to The Learning Company, which Mattel acquired in 1999. O’Leary is also into politics and campaigned to be the leader of the Conservative Party of Canada in 2017.

Kevin was born on Terence Thomas Kevin O’Leary on 9th July 1954 in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. His mother, Georgette, was a small business owner and of Lebanese descent.

Similarly, his father was of Irish descent and was a salesman. Leary grew up with his brother Shane O’Leary in the Town of Mount Royal, Quebec. O’Leary also holds Irish citizenship and passport due to his parental heritage.

Kevin’s father was an alcoholic, which led to divorce when Kevin was just a child.

Soon after the divorce, his father died when O’Leary was only seven years old. After his father’s death, his mother married George Kanawaty, an economist who worked with the UN’s International Labor Organization.

Because of his stepfather’s work, the family frequently moved to different countries, including Ethiopia, Tunisia, Cyprus, and Cambodia.

Investments

During his MBA days, O’Leary did an internship at Nabisco and worked as an assistant brand manager for the company’s cat food brand.

He thanks this experience for helping him develop skills that aided his later success.

After quitting Nabisco, Kevin embarked on his career as a television producer. He also co-founded the TV production company Special Event Television (SET) with two former fellow MBA classmates.

Kevin’s share of the partnership was eventually acquired by one of these partners for $25,000.

He then founded SoftKey, a software company, in 1986 in Toronto.

One of the sponsors chickened out of the project, which led Kevin to invest the $25,000 SET share money and an additional $10,000 investment loan from his mother to establish the company.

The company was a distributor and publisher of CD-ROM-based PC software for Macintosh and Windows computers.

The company got bigger and acquired several rival companies.

However, Mattel acquired the whole company for $4.2 billion in 1999. When the sales declined, Kevin quit the company.

Kevin also invested in the company Now Storage in 2003.

The company develops a climate-controlled storage facility which is now Canada’s third-largest operator of storage services. The company is in eleven cities serving such major companies as Merck and Pfizer.

However, in March 2007, the company was acquired for $110 million.

Furthermore, Kevin joined the advisory board of Genstar Capital in March 2007, which is an equity firm that focuses on investing in industrial technologies, business services, and software and healthcare services.

Genstar Capital selected O’Leary to its Strategic Advisory Board to seek new investment opportunities for its $1.2 billion fund.

Book Publications

O’Leary published his first book, Cold Hard Truth: On Business, Money & Life, in September 2011, in which he shares his opinions on relationships, investing, money, and life.

Similarly, in 2012, he published a sequel named The Cold Hard Truth on Men, Women, and Money: 50 Common Money Mistakes and How to Fix Them.

The book focused on financial literateness and financial education as a foundation for achieving wealth.

In 2013 O’Leary released a follow-up in which he covers subjects relating to essential life choices: education, careers, marriage and family, and retirement. Moreover, the book is named Cold Hard Truth on Family, Kids, and money.

He discusses the hindrances of raising a family while working to provide financial security for them and advises on developing financial literateness in family members, saving and investing money, and handling debt and credit.

Career

Kevin’s mother was a skilled investor who invested her weekly paycheck in dividend-paying stocks.

However, she kept the investments secret, and Kevin only discovered his mother’s skill as an investor after her death through the will.

Kevin went to St. George’s School and Stanstead College, both in Quebec, Canada. Moreover, Kevin wanted to become a photographer but later chose to attend university as per the advice of his stepfather.

The Canadian Businessman developed an interest in investing and business. Furthermore, in 1980, he received an honorary MBA degree in entrepreneurship from the Ivey Business School at the University of Western Ontario.

Similarly, in 1977, he received an honorary bachelor’s degree in environmental studies and psychology from the University of Waterloo.

Kevin officially entered the Conservative leadership race following months of public speculation on 18th January 2017.

The press compared him to former President Donald Trump during his campaign. Both were businessmen who gained recognition through reality TV and ran on platforms that included lowering taxes and regulations.

On 26th April 2017, he officially left the leadership race. Apart from that, Kevin’s been married to Linda since 1990, with a brief two-year separation in 2011.

Moreover, Kevin worked as a co-host for the Discovery Channel’s Discovery Project Earth in 2008. The show focused on innovative ways to reverse climate change.
