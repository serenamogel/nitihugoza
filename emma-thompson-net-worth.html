Emma Thompson Net Worth

Emma Thompson is an English actress, writer and activist who has made a significant contribution to the entertainment industry. Early Life and Education

Emma Thompson was born on April 15, 1959, in Paddington, London, England. Her parents were both actors, and she grew up in a household filled with culture and art. Thompson attended primary school at Camden School for Girls before moving on to study English Literature at Newnham College, Cambridge University.

Career

Thompson’s professional acting career began in the 1980s, where she started off with minor roles on stage and television. Her big break came in 1985 when she was cast in the West End production of “Me and My Girl.” This led to her getting more significant roles in television and film, including her memorable performance in “Howard’s End,” which won her an Academy Award for Best Actress.

Since then, Thompson has appeared in several critically acclaimed movies such as “Sense and Sensibility,” “Love Actually,” “Nanny McPhee,” and “Saving Mr. Banks.” She has also lent her voice in various animated films, including her role as the voice of Queen Elinor in Disney-Pixar’s “Brave.”

Apart from her acting career, Thompson is also known for being a successful screenwriter and producer. She wrote and starred in the movie “Nanny McPhee,” and penned the screenplay for the award-winning adaptation of Jane Austen’s “Sense and Sensibility,” which won her a BAFTA Award.

Personal Life

Emma Thompson married actor and director Kenneth Branagh in 1989. However, the couple divorced in 1995, leading to Thompson’s public battle with depression. Later on, Thompson married actor and producer Greg Wise in 2003, and the couple has two children together.

Thompson has also been an active advocate for human rights and social justice issues. She is a prominent supporter of the UK-based human rights organization, Justice, and campaigned for the release of Burmese opposition leader Aung San Suu Kyi.
