Biography of Rob Schneider

Rob Schneider is an American actor, comedian, and writer, known for his work on Saturday Night Live, as well as for his roles in various comedy films. Schneider’s early career included appearances on HBO’s 13th Annual Young Comedians special and The David Letterman Show. However, it was his work on Saturday Night Live from 1990-1994 that launched his career. During his time on the show, Schneider created several memorable characters, including Richard Laymer, the copy machine guy, and the “Makin’ Copies” catchphrase which became a pop culture phenomenon.

Aside from his work on SNL, Schneider has also written for several films, beginning with the screenplay for Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo in 1999. He went on to write, produce, and star in several successful comedies, including The Hot Chick (2002), The Benchwarmers (2006), and Grown Ups (2010).
