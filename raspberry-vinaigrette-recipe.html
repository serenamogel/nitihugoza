All you need is a food processor and about 10 minutes of prep time, and you’ll have an amazingly creamy raspberry vinaigrette. You can find the printable recipe at the end of this post but let’s take a quick look at what you’ll need:

  • fresh raspberries – The base of any raspberry vinaigrette.
  • honey – I like to use raw honey for the flavor.
  • garlic powder – Fresh garlic could be used in place of the powder.
  • white wine vinegar – You can try swapping in apple cider vinegar for a sweeter flavor, but I prefer the taste of the white wine vinegar.
  • olive oil – This will help the consistency of the vinaigrette by emulsifying. I like to use a light olive oil but use what you prefer.
  • salt – to taste.
  • pepper – Freshly ground pepper is my go to for the most flavor. 

How To Make Homemade Raspberry Vinaigrette

Under 10 minutes and you’re on your way to a beautiful, fresh and fruity salad dressing! Let’s see how easy it is to make:

  • In a food processor (or blender, whichever you have on hand), combine the fresh raspberries, garlic powder, honey, and vinegar.
  • Process on medium high for about 30 seconds until the mixture is smooth. 
  • Add in the olive oil to the mixture and process for another 30 seconds until emulsified and the mixture has thinned out to your preferred consistency. 
  • Do a quick taste test and add in additional spices if needed.
  • Process for another 10 seconds. Continue this step until you’ve reached the flavor profile you’re looking for. 
  • Transfer your raspberry vinaigrette to a mason jar or airtight container and place in the refrigerator until you’re ready to use. 

    • FAQs

    How long will the raspberry vinaigrette stay fresh?

    Store the homemade raspberry vinaigrette dressing in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.

    How healthy is the raspberry vinaigrette?

    Salad dressings that use oil and vinegar as their base are typically much healthier options as opposed to heavier salads with a creamy base. This recipe also leaves out the refined sugars and instead uses honey to add sweetness, keeping the calories lower.

    Helpful Tips

    Here are a few helpful tips to take into consideration:

    • If you are serving this at a special event, I don’t recommend tossing the dressing into the salad, but rather allowing your guests to serve themselves. 
    • When storing the vinaigrette, keep it in a truly airtight container. If the lid is too loose and extra air gets in, the vinaigrette could form a film at the top. Mason jars are tried and true!
    • Remember, your salad dressing is only as good as the ingredients you use to make it. Use fresh, blemish free raspberries for the best results.

    Variations To Try

    This dressing is SO delicious but here are some easy variations to try if you want to mix things up:

    • You can substitute balsamic vinegar in this recipe, however you will not get the beautiful bright pink color. It’s delicious though!
    • Use strawberries or blueberries instead of raspberries.
    • Try different seasonings like onion powder to enhance the flavors in the vinaigrette.
    • Use agave nectar instead of honey.
    • Add poppy seeds.
    • Add some fresh lemon juice for a freshness and vibrancy.

    Raspberry Vinaigrette

    This Raspberry Vinaigrette recipe is perfectly sweet and tart and is a delicious addition to any salad recipe! Quick and easy to make with just a handful of ingredients! A summer staple!

    Course Condiment Cuisine American Keyword raspberry vinaigrette, raspberry vinaigrette dressing, raspberry vinaigrette recipe Prep Time 10 minutes minutes Cook Time 0 minutes minutes Servings 8 Calories 129kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 6 ounces raspberries
    • 1 tablespoon honey or agave nectar
    • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
    • 3 tablespoons White Wine Vinegar
    • 7 tablespoons olive oil I used extra virgin
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • freshly ground pepper to taste

    Instructions

    • In a food processor (or blender) combine the raspberries, garlic powder, honey and vinegar. Process on medium high for 30 seconds until mixture is smooth.

    • Add olive oil and process for another 30 seconds until emulsified.

    • Add in spices and process for an additional 10 seconds.

    • Taste and adjust spices if necessary.

    • Transfer vinaigrette to a mason jar and refrigerate until ready to use.

    Notes

    You can substitute balsamic vinegar in this recipe, however you will not get the beautiful bright pink color. It’s delicious though!
