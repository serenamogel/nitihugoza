How did Scott Hall start his Professional Career?

He started wrestling professionally in 1984 in South Carolina, and in the CWF (Championship Wrestling Florida), he wrestled for the NWA (National Wrestling Alliance).

He was signed to the AWA (American Wrestling Association) organization in 1985 after they took notice of him. In 1987, he participated in the NJPW (New Japan Pro-Wrestling) league. About one year before AWA went bankrupt, he left the business in 1989 to join NWA.

He participated in a vignette and a house show in June of the same year as a part of the NWA’s new program to develop fresh talent. In July, he debuted in professional wrestling for WCW. He tried to participate in a WWF Wrestling Challenge in 1990 but was unsuccessful. Then he joined the NJPW once more.

Also, he competed as “Texas Scott” in the German CWA’s “Catch Cup 90” league. He participated in the WCW’s Puerto Rico promotion in 1991. He returned to WCW as “The Diamond Studd” in April of that year.
